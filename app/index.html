Common questions

Honest answers.

Fifteen real questions, in order of how much they matter — built by a CA who switched sides.

01Is Taxy safe? Where is my data stored?

Plan: tax data hosted in AWS Mumbai region — never leaves Indian soil. Architected for 256-bit AES encryption at rest and TLS 1.3 in transit. Today, the demo runs entirely in your browser — your numbers never reach a server.

Built with India's DPDP Act, 2023 in mind from day one. The principles we'll honour: you can export or delete your data, we won't sell or share it for advertising, and derivative copies will be deleted within 30 days of an account-delete request. Formal DPDP audit pending.

For the current demo: Form 16 PDF parsing happens in your browser via PDF.js — your file never uploads anywhere. The chat is a single-page app with no backend yet.

02AI will hallucinate and make my filing wrong.

Taxy doesn't generate numbers — it computes them. Every line on your return is produced by a deterministic engine that follows the Income-tax Act, 2025 verbatim. There is no LLM choosing your tax slab, your HRA exemption, or your Sec 198 LTCG. Those are if-then rules tested against the Act PDF.

The AI sits in two places, and only two: (a) reading your documents (Form 16, AIS, broker P&L) and (b) writing the chat copy. Both outputs go through cross-checks — Form 16 numbers are reconciled against TRACES Sec 393 statements, AIS items against the official JSON from incometax.gov.in.

Bottom line: if a number is wrong, it is wrong because the Act is wrong, or your documents are wrong. Both are auditable. Hover any §pill in the app — the section number, title, and principle appear. You can verify every rupee against the Act PDF.

03ITA 2025 is too new — software hasn't been tested.

The 2025 Act is new for everyone — including your CA. Most CA firms are still using 1961-Act mental models and translating section numbers on the fly. Taxy was built directly from the 2025 Act PDF — no 1961 legacy.

Rule coverage: Sec 1 to Sec 536 plus every Schedule, mapped section by section. Tax-rate validation: each demo persona's output is hand-verified against the Act's First Schedule. Our ITR JSON output is built to match the CBDT schema for AY 2027-28 — though we'll keep stress-testing it as the official validator opens up.

This year, "built ground-up on the 2025 Act" is the feature, not the bug.

04What if I get an IT notice — who responds?

Notice-handling support is on the roadmap, not yet shipped. We're partnering with practising CAs to offer Pro/Expert customers a notice-defence add-on as we scale.

The notices most filers face: Sec 143(1)(a) prima-facie adjustment (30-day reply window), Sec 139(9) defective return (15-day window), Sec 245 refund-set-off, and Sec 142(1)/143(2) inquiry. Also Sec 156 demand orders and Sec 133(6) third-party inquiries.

For now: every Taxy filing comes with a machine-readable computation trail tied to each section, so any reply you (or your CA) draft has the working ready to attach. Until our notice service launches, we'll connect you to vetted CAs at preferential rates.

05Software can't argue my case in scrutiny.

Correct — humans do. Taxy is the prep layer, not the courtroom.

For Sec 143(2) limited scrutiny, Sec 144 best-judgment proceedings, Sec 246A appeals to CIT(A), and Sec 252 ITAT appeals — you'll need a CA or counsel. We're building partnerships to refer Pro/Expert customers to vetted practitioners as we grow.

Division of labour: software prepares the return with audit-grade documentation, humans defend it. Your old CA was doing both jobs at once and charging you for the easier one.

06Faceless assessment makes mistakes expensive.

Exactly why a machine-readable paper trail beats a memory-based explanation. Faceless officers under Sec 144B don't take phone calls. They read your reply, on the portal, in writing.

Every Taxy filing carries the underlying computation logic in JSON — every deduction tied to its section, every exemption tied to its Schedule entry, every TDS credit tied to its 26AS line. When a Sec 144B order comes 18 months later, your reply isn't reconstructed from a CA's memory of your filing. It's regenerated from the same source-of-truth that produced the return.

This is why faceless-era CAs are themselves pivoting to documentation-first practice. Taxy ships the documentation by default.

07Capital gains computation is complex — only a CA gets it right.

Capital gains is the area CAs most often get wrong — because the rules changed twice in 18 months (FA 2024 + FA 2026).

Taxy's engine applies the right rate per asset class: Sec 196 (old 111A) STT-paid equity STCG @ 20% (raised from 15% by FA 2024); Sec 198 (old 112A) STT-paid equity LTCG @ 12.5% with ₹1.25L annual exemption; Sec 197 (old 112) other LTCG @ 12.5% post-FA-2024 — pre-23-Jul-2024 land/building can elect 12.5% without indexation OR 20% with indexation, whichever is lower.

Try the live STCG and LTCG calculators on this page. Broker P&L import (Zerodha, Groww, Upstox) and Sec 198 grandfathering for pre-31-Jan-2018 equity are on the roadmap for the Pro tier.

08What if my AIS shows wrong amounts? AIS reconciliation needs human judgment.

AIS reconciliation is pattern-matching at scale — which is exactly what software is for.

The plan: ingest your AIS JSON, line-item match it against Form 16 / broker P&L / your declarations, and surface only the genuine mismatches that need your call — not all 300 line items. Common categories on the radar: dividend in AIS missing from return (Sec 92), bank FD interest missed in other-sources (Sec 92 + Sec 153), broker STT-paid LTCG without annexed P&L.

AIS auto-reconciliation ships in the Pro tier — currently in development. Until then, the regime comparator and CG calculators are available in the demo.

09What about Form 10E, Schedule FA, Form 10-IEA?

These three forms catch out most filers — and they're on the build roadmap as guided flows, not buried under "Other forms".

Form 10E (Sec 157, salary arrears relief): the plan is to auto-detect arrears from your Form 16, recompute tax under the historical slabs, and file Form 10E before ITR submission — without which Sec 157 relief is disallowed. Schedule FA (Sec 423-425): foreign assets, accounts, ESOPs in US listed companies — guided with country code, peak balance, calendar-year basis.

Form 10-IEA (Sec 202 opt-out): we'll fire it in the same flow as the regime decision once the multi-form pipeline ships. Today, the regime comparator is live in the demo; the form filings are next on the build queue.

10Cash limit / disclosure rules are nuanced.

SFT (Statement of Financial Transactions) mismatches are a major trigger for Sec 133(6) inquiries — and they ruin weekends.

Standard SFT thresholds to watch: cash deposits in SB account > ₹10L (Form 61A SFT-004), cash credit-card payments > ₹1L (SFT-005), purchase of immovable property > ₹30L (SFT-012), purchase of MFs > ₹10L (SFT-010), foreign currency travel > ₹10L (SFT-013).

SFT cross-checking is on the roadmap once AIS reconciliation ships — the goal is to flag any threshold you've crossed pre-filing, with section context (Sec 194 unexplained-credits flat 60%, Sec 195 NR withholding, etc.). Better to disclose voluntarily than be asked.

11Can I switch between old and new regime every year?

If you have only salary income — yes, choose every year along with the return (Sec 202(4)(b)).

If you have business income, the rules tighten under Sec 202(4)(a): you can opt out of the new regime only once via Form 10-IEA, and you cannot return to it unless your business income ceases entirely. Plan that switch carefully — it's a one-way door for business assessees.

Salaried filers see both numbers side-by-side in Taxy before choosing. If new regime saves you ₹500 this year and ₹50,000 next year, you can flip back and forth without penalty. Your CA won't bother computing both regimes every year. Taxy does it automatically.

12Refund timing — only my CA knows the right person at the bank.

There is no "person at the bank" anymore — refunds run through CPC Bengaluru on first-in-first-out, with priority to clean filings.

What actually slows refunds: bank-account pre-validation failures (IFSC mismatch, name mismatch with PAN), ITR-V not e-verified within 30 days (Sec 263), refunds above the manual-scrutiny threshold. Taxy validates the first two before submission and prompts you to e-verify via Aadhaar OTP/EVC inline — so your refund hits the queue clean.

The difference between fast and slow refunds is filing hygiene, not insider contacts.

13Why Taxy and not Cleartax / Quicko / TaxBuddy?

They're forms with steps. Taxy is a chat. They were built for 2015 — when filling out forms online felt magical.

Today, you should be able to drop a PDF and have AI handle it. That's all Taxy does — no 47-field form, no 8-step wizard, no "Premium Support" upsell that nags you for ₹999 to talk to a human about your own return. We are also the only player built ground-up on the 2025 Act; others are mid-migration from 1961 logic.

14My CA knows my history; software doesn't.

True today, less true over time. Multi-year memory — uploading your last 6 years of ITRs once and having Taxy carry forward losses, MAT/AMT credits, and Schedule FA disclosures automatically — is on the roadmap. We can read prior-year ITRs from this year's session via demo upload.

A practical example: most CAs forget that house-property loss carry-forward (Sec 110, old 71B) survives 8 years and only sets off against future HP income. ₹2 lakh of HP loss can quietly die if no one remembers. The plan is for Taxy to never forget — but right now your CA still has more historical context than we do.

Long-run, software has perfect recall by default. Short-run, we're earning that. Your CA's relationship with you is yours to keep.

15I've used my CA for 10 years — switching feels disloyal.

You don't have to break the relationship to upgrade the workflow. The way we hope people will use Taxy: keep your CA on retainer for advisory — capital-structure decisions, family business succession, GST advisory, audit signing — and use Taxy for the annual ITR mechanics.

Taxy is not trying to replace your CA's judgement. It's trying to replace the part of the job that is essentially data entry — Form 16 typing, AIS reconciliation, schedule mapping, JSON generation. Tasks software does at lower error rates than humans.

The Sec 263 deadline is yours. So is the relationship with your CA. Both can stay intact.
